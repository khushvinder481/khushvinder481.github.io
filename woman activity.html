Women Empowerment

In our society, women are the embodiment of power and creativity. In tribal society, women not only take care of the house but also contribute to farming, animal husbandry, and forest work. Even in marketplaces, women play a dominant role as both shoppers and sellers. Women have historically held important positions in village governance in some regions.

Structured women empowerment work began with Ms. Leelatai Paradkarji in 1973. Over the years, numerous conferences and projects were organized, including the All India Women’s Conferences in Bhilai (1985) and Ranchi (2007). Today, Kalyan Ashram supports women empowerment at all levels, from village committees to national level, and many women workers are engaged full-time in these initiatives.

Women actively contribute in education, health, self-reliance, self-protection, sports, hostels for girls, sanskar centers, saving groups, and publication work. They manage projects and ensure awareness in the forest dwelling community and society at large. Their contributions are invaluable and part of the ongoing organizational plans.
